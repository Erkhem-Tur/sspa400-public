ALC Book 5 Lesson 1 — Body, Doctor Visit, Food Safety + Which/One/Or

Mission: Talk about body parts, sickness, food choices/allergies, and service/doctor situations.

ALC focus: Doctor dialogue, body parts, skin/muscles/bones, which/one, connector or, dates/order words, scanning.

SSPA context: SSPA medical support, first aid, restaurant/hotel service, food safety, allergies, polite service questions.

How this lesson integrates ALC + SSPA handbook

1. Learn ALC language

Vocabulary and grammar are taught from the ALC lesson.

2. Add operational context

Students apply the same grammar to protection, checkpoint, duty, service, and report situations.

3. Perform professionally

Every task ends with a role-play, duty report, or service conversation.

Grammar explanation + operational examples

Which one / ones

Which one hurts? The left one. Which shoes are yours? The black ones.

Нэр үгийг давтахгүй one/ones хэрэглэнэ.

Or for choices

Does your head or throat hurt? Tea or water? Gluten-free or regular?

Сонголт: эсвэл.

Doctor question frame

What seems to be the problem? How long have you had these symptoms?

Эмчийн асуултын загвар.

Order words

first, second, third, next, then, finally for instructions and scanning tasks.

Дараалал, заавар хэлэхэд ашиглана.

Scenario cards

  1. Doctor visit after duty: cough, sore throat, headache
  2. Restaurant service: allergy and menu questions
  3. First aid report: knee/arm/hand injury
  4. Scan a menu and choose safe food for a guest

Teacher modeling sequence

  1. Teacher says the model twice: slow → natural speed.
  2. Class repeats chorally.
  3. Students identify vocabulary + grammar.
  4. Pairs substitute rank, place, time, item, and purpose.
  5. Students perform without reading.
  6. Teacher corrects: pronunciation → grammar → professional tone.
Багш эхлээд загварлан хэлнэ, сурагчид нийтээр давтана, дараа нь өөрсдийн нөхцөлд сольж ярьж гүйцэтгэнэ.

Model dialogue

Open model

Officer: Good morning, sir/ma’am. How can I help you?

Visitor/Colleague: Good morning. I am here for the training/event at 0900.

Officer: May I check your ID, permission, or badge please?

Visitor/Colleague: Yes, here it is.

Officer: Thank you. Please follow the instructions.

Quick quiz

  1. Doctor, I am ____. Answer: sick
  2. Which ____ hurts, the left one or the right one? Answer: one
  3. Do you have gluten-free ____? Answer: dishes
  4. Does your knee ____ your foot hurt? Answer: or
  5. Put a ____ on the injury. Answer: bandage

Homework / LMS submission

Submit: 10 sentences + one 8–10 line dialogue + one short duty/service report. Use at least 12 vocabulary words, 3 SSPA handbook phrases, and 2 grammar targets.
