Background / Танилцуулга

The GSPR (Groupe de Sécurité de la Présidence de la République — Presidential Security Group) is the elite unit responsible for the close protection of the President of the French Republic and his or her family. Their entire professional life is organised around the Élysée Palace and the schedule of the head of state — every deployment, every operation, every shift begins and ends with what the President requires.

Mission & Daily Operations / Эрхэм зорилго ба өдөр тутмын ажиллагаа

Before any public appearance or official visit, GSPR agents conduct thorough advance reconnaissance. They visit the location in advance to assess potential threats and decide on protective measures: entry and exit routes, exfiltration routes, crowd management zones, and emergency contingencies.

During transport, the President travels in a motorcade — a coordinated convoy of multiple vehicles. Routes are varied for security reasons, and crucially, only the security chief knows in advance which route will actually be used. This prevents any possibility of a predetermined ambush.

At public events and open-air appearances, agents form a tight protective perimeter around the President, continuously scanning for threats. Throughout all operations, teams maintain constant real-time communication to adapt instantly to changing situations and coordinate potential extractions if needed.

Team Structure & Staffing / Багийн бүтэц ба бүрэлдэхүүн

The GSPR consists of exactly 78 agents — 39 from the Police Nationale (National Police) and 39 from the Gendarmerie Nationale (Military Police). This deliberate 50/50 balance ensures that two institutional cultures — civilian policing and military discipline — are permanently represented in the unit.

Candidates are drawn exclusively from two elite feeder units: the GIGN (Groupe d'Intervention de la Gendarmerie Nationale) for gendarmes, and the SDLP (Service de la Défense et de la Protection) for police officers.

Key Competencies / Гол чадамж

GSPR agents must demonstrate four core professional qualities at all times:

  • Stress and pressure management — maintaining composure in high-risk situations
  • Reactivity — rapid, decisive response to unexpected events
  • Discretion — absolute confidentiality regarding the protectee's movements and security measures
  • Excellent physical condition — meeting demanding fitness standards throughout service
Recruitment & Selection / Элсэлт ба сонгон шалгаруулалт

Entry into the GSPR follows two distinct career tracks, both of which are exceptionally demanding.

Police Track: Candidates first join the Police Nationale by passing a competitive concours (civil service examination) at either the Gardien de la Paix level ( BAC qualification required) or the Officier de Police level (BAC+3 required). After completing police academy training and gaining at least two years of operational experience, officers may apply for selection into the SDLP protection service. SDLP selection tests cover sports performance, marksmanship, psychotechnical assessments, driving evaluation, professional knowledge, language and IT testing, and a jury interview.

Gendarmerie Track: Candidates pass the gendarmerie concours and complete academy training of 12–24 months. They must then meet the demanding GIGN eligibility criteria: at least 4 years of service, specific rank, and age between 24 and 34. The GIGN selection process — famously one of the toughest in the world — has a success rate of only 10%. It involves a week-long initial assessment, an 8-week pre-selection stage, 12 months of operational training, and a final 2-month close-protection specialisation.

Final GSPR Selection: Candidates shortlisted from both tracks face one final week-long assessment at the Élysée Palace, directed by the Presidential Security Directorate. Those who pass receive specialised training in combat techniques, exfiltration protocols, and hostage scenarios.

Salary & Career / Цалин хөлс ба ажлын зам

Compensation varies by institution, rank, and experience. In 2024, entry-level monthly salaries range from approximately €2,145 (gendarmerie non-commissioned officer) to €2,780 (gendarmerie officer). Experienced GSPR members earn substantially more, supplemented by availability allowances reflecting the extraordinary demands of protecting the head of state around the clock.
