United States Secret Service: Workforce Challenges and Protective Mission GAO Report — Adapted
Background / Танилцуулга

The United States Secret Service (USSS) is a federal law enforcement agency within the Department of Homeland Security. Established in 1865, the agency carries out a dual mission: protecting the nation's protectees — including the President, Vice President, and visiting heads of state — and investigating financial crimes such as counterfeiting and cybercrime. As of 2024, the agency employs approximately 8,300 personnel across 136 domestic field offices and 20 overseas locations.

Workforce Structure / Ажилтны бүтэц

The USSS workforce consists of two primary categories. Special Agents (SAs) conduct both criminal investigations and protective operations, while Uniformed Division (UD) Officers provide physical security at protected facilities, including the White House Complex and diplomatic missions. Supporting these two groups are administrative and technical support personnel who manage logistics, communications, and specialized functions.

According to GAO findings, the agency has faced persistent attrition challenges. The separation rate for Special Agents has consistently exceeded the agency's ability to recruit and onboard qualified replacements. The eligibility requirements for entry — including a security clearance, physical fitness standards, and a multi-stage selection process — further constrain the candidate pool.

Training Pipeline / Сургалтын дамжлага

Newly hired agents must complete an extensive onboarding and training pipeline lasting 18 months or more. This includes the Criminal Investigator Training Program at the Federal Law Enforcement Training Centers (FLETC) and the agency's own in-house training curriculum. During this period, field training officers (FTOs) mentor recruits and certify their operational readiness. GAO noted that resource constraints in the training pipeline have at times delayed class intakes, compounding existing staffing shortfalls.

Protective Operations / Хамгаалалтын ажиллагаа

The agency's protective details operate around the clock, providing 24/7 coverage to designated protectees. Prior to any visit, agents conduct advance work — surveying venues, coordinating with local law enforcement counterparts, and establishing security perimeters. The Counter Assault Team (CAT) and counter-sniper units provide tactical protective support during high-risk movements such as motorcades and public events.

The agency also manages National Special Security Events (NSSEs) such as presidential inaugurations, State of the Union addresses, and international summits. These events require multi-agency coordination and the temporary deployment of large numbers of personnel.

Key Challenges / Гол бэрхшээлүүд

GAO identified several critical challenges affecting USSS workforce well-being and organizational effectiveness. Excessive overtime — sometimes exceeding 20 hours per week — has led to cumulative fatigue among agents and officers. Surveys indicated that a significant proportion of personnel reported work-life imbalance and professional burnout as primary reasons for separation decisions.

GAO recommended that the USSS develop a comprehensive strategic workforce plan that addresses retention, recruitment, and staffing levels in alignment with operational requirements. The agency concurred with the majority of recommendations and outlined planned corrective actions.
